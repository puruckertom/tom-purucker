Applied Research

I am a research ecologist at an EPA research lab in Athens, Georgia. I work with other biologists, chemists, engineers, statisticians, and programmers conducting applied research on environmental exposures and effects of pesticides.

Federal law on pesticides

The US Congress has passed a number of laws concerning the registration and use of pesticides. The EPA regulates the use of pesticides under these federal statutes. Approved pesticides carry product labels that provide information on how to safely handle and use pesticides.

Pesticide laws and regulations

Estimating exposure and effects

Data about pesticides are submitted to the EPA, this data, combined with a number of environmental exposure and effect models, are used to determine if a pesticide is to be registered and how their use is restricted. We have reprogrammed a number of these models so they can be run as web applications.

Challenging models with data

An important part of using models to estimate real-world phenomena is to compare model predictions to observations. We collect data on amphibians and honeybees to test the predictions of pesticide models using both hypothesis testing and likelihood estimation approaches.
